A GASTRONOMIC JOURNEY IN VIETNAM – part 4, Hoi An

Sep 21, 2015

After Hanoi, Ho Chi Minh City, and Hue, here comes the last part of our series, Hoi An and its multi-influences cuisine. Vietnam made its reputation as a popular tourism destination worldwide through scenic roads, legendary landscapes and a very rich and diverse culture. These are still the main assets for the country to attract international visitors. But a new trend is emerging in the tourism sphere, we call it experiential travel. People do travel to see things and to feel away from home. But they focus more and more on the experiences to be lived. They do not visit the country anymore, they experience it. And in Vietnam, gastronomy is certainly one of the first things to put on your to-experience list. It is blessed with rich forest, limestone panoramas, magnificent rice terraces and breathtaking scenery.

Brighten the days of Nam Vi kids.m

Sep 08, 2015

Handspan recently connected with an active group of young fellows carrying social projects in the poorest regions of North Vietnam. The project is called Anh Sang Nui Rung [Lighten mountains and forests] and gather around 70 active members. They already built from scratch two schools in very remote areas. After they completed the second one in May 2015 in a secluded region at the far Northwest of Vietnam, they realized that kids could hardly attend the afternoon classes because they have to knock about the forest searching for food. These families have no running water, no electricity and very scarce food supply.
