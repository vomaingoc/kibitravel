Halong Bay Order

  • Overview
  • Itinerary
  • Prices
This little (but extendable) loop takes you along a limestone mountain range stretching from the western boundaries of the country to the legendary seascape of Halong. Sceneries change every day, from green paddies of Mai Chau valley, through the forest of Pu Luong nature reserve and the towering limestone peaks of Ninh Binh, to Halong and Bai Tu Long bays. If you want the pleasure to last longer, contact our travel consultants to add an extra day at any of those destinations.

DESTINATION


Mai Hich village in the Mai Chau valley
The nature reserve of Pu Luong
The so-called Inland Halong bay
The original Halong bay and its twin sister Bai Tu Long

HIGHLIGHTS


Leisure cycling across Mai Chau valley
Trekking to remote ethnic village for homestay overnight
Fantastic, changing sceneries
A comfortable cruise on the less traveled routes of Halong and Bai Tu Long bays

DAY 1 HANOI – MAI CHAU

Meals: lunch and dinner | Accommodation: Traditional guesthouse on stilts

Departure from your hotel at 8.00am. On the morning drive to Mai Chau, we'll leave the city behind and pass through craggy, mountainous landscape. We drive up a massive pass and end in the mountainous circus of Mai Chau. On arrival in the small remote village of Mai Hich, we'll check in to the traditional guesthouse of a Thai ethnic minority family, wooden and on stilts, like we love them. In the afternoon, you have the choice of hiking or cycling around the green hills letting your guide point out and explain the wonders along the way. If the weather is good, we can also go check out the nice cascades hidden somewhere, few kilometers behind the village. Afterwards, we return for dinner and overnight in the stilt house with our friendly Thai hosts.

DAY 2 MAI CHAU – PU LUONG

Meals: breakfast, lunch and dinner | Accommodation: Homestay

We leave our hosts after breakfast and drive East for about 15 kilometers before entering the Pu Luong nature reserve. The car will take us on a small road across the first half of the reserve. Many opportunities to stop and enjoy some views on deep valleys sculpted with terraced rice fields. We are surrounded by forested limestone mountains. But enough road for today. We get down the car and walk on a single trail down a valley for about one hour. A breathtaking hike that leads us to a small village, where we have lunch in a home.
In the afternoon, we step into the wild with an awesome hike on trails across the second half of the reserve to reach its Western edge. The hike is not too hard and lasts 3 to 4 hours. It makes it an intermediate level trek. Plan to bring along good shoes and batteries for your camera. After a demanding afternoon, the reward is unique and offered by Mother Nature herself. Refresh and swim in the cascades nearby our overnight village. We are welcomed in a large house on stilts by a friendly Thai family. Dinner and overnight at the homestay.

DAY 3 PU LUONG – TAM COC

Meals: breakfast, lunch and dinner | Accommodation: Friendly guesthouse

After breakfast, we say farewell to our hosts and hike for about 2 hours across a valley until we reach the little road where our car is waiting. We have a lunch en-route, on the way to the Inland Halong bay. We arrive at destination in the mid-afternoon. Just the time to drop the luggage and there we go for our little exploration. You can choose to hop on bicycles, or to sit in an ox cart to journey across the Halong-like sceneries of Tam Coc and learn more about this typical region of the Red River delta. Later in the afternoon, when most tourists are gone and the sun is blowing orange light, we get on a local rowed sampan and discover the scenic waterways of the area. We come back to the hotel in the center of the small Tam Coc town. Dinner then evening at leisure.
